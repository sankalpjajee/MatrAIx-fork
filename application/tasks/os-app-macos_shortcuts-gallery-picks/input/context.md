# Shortcuts on macOS

Shortcuts is Apple's built-in automation app. It lets you chain actions from
different apps into reusable one-tap workflows — things like sending a message,
resizing photos, converting files, or building multi-step routines that fire
from the menu bar, Dock, or Siri.

The **Gallery** inside Shortcuts is a curated collection of ready-made shortcuts
organized into categories. You can browse them to get a feel for what
automations are available and add any that fit your daily life.

When browsing the Gallery, think about:

- **Your daily routine** — do any shortcuts save you time on things you already
  do (e.g. commute info, morning playlists, quick notes)?
- **Your work** — could a shortcut help with files, meetings, messages, or
  other work tasks?
- **Your hobbies and interests** — are there shortcuts for things you enjoy
  (e.g. photos, fitness, reading, music)?
- **Convenience vs. complexity** — would you actually use a shortcut regularly,
  or is it a novelty you'd forget about?

Pick shortcuts that genuinely fit *you*, not ones that just sound impressive.
